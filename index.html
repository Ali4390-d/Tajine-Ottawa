Our Story

Where Morocco
Meets Ottawa

A kitchen built on memory. A table set for strangers who become family.

Tajine Ottawa was born from Chef Amal Essarghini's longing for the kitchens of Fès — the slow afternoons, the first press of cardamom against stone, the way her grandmother turned lamb shanks in clay until the bone gave itself to the broth. When she moved to Ottawa in 2014, she brought three family tajines in her suitcase. The restaurant on Dalhousie Street is what grew around them.

Every dish here is a small inheritance. The b'stilla is folded in the same pattern Amal learned at thirteen. The mint tea is poured from the height her father insisted on — high enough that the leaves open, never so high that the cup cools. We are a Moroccan house in the ByWard Market, and the door is open Tuesday through Sunday, from five until ten.
